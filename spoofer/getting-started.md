**Step 5: Disable Secure Boot**

**Identifying your motherboard:**

1. Press **Windows + R** to open the Run dialog.
2. Type **msinfo32** and press Enter.
3. Look for the following fields:
   * **BaseBoard Manufacturer**
   * **BaseBoard Product**
   * **BaseBoard Version**<br>
4. Video's how to disable Secure Boot:\
   \- [Asus](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tnOHi0w77bU)\
   \- [MSI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LbeCV9NL5ls)\
   \- [Dell](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=brXcxmdWU9Q)\
   \- [AsRock](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v-34aIjbtvo)\
   \- [GigaByte](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=63t2TYHmBgc) <br>
5. To open BIOS&#x20;
6. Restart your computer and enter the BIOS/UEFI settings (usually by pressing F2, F10, DEL, or ESC during startup).&#x20;
7. Find the **Secure Boot** setting in your BIOS menu.&#x20;
8. Go to Advanced mode (Not for all the same)
9. Set it to **Disabled**. This option is usually in either the **Security** tab, the **Boot** tab, or the **Authentication** tab.
10. For Asus delete Secure Boot Keys
11. Save the changes and exit the BIOS/UEFI.
